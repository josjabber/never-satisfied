Never Satisfied

By Josie Jaback

Kick, score, goal... It was all a dream As I look up into the stands, all I see is celebration Mundane office walls overtake me The joy that comes from winning a game never fades, even after years of playing Depression consumed me over the years Forever grateful for choosing soccer in college, I would have never got to this point had I not A useless college degree I make my way home after the game, kids and husband waiting for me. Bottles waiting for me at home I unwind, curl up in bed and let out a sigh. The day is finally over A life of stagnancy was one I never suspected as a child A life of fame was not one I saw plausible. Never satisfied All of these games, the same result, what if I want more? At least I am stable, at least I am alive What will be enough I have everything I could ask for, money, fame, what about love. I have all I could need, drinks, money, stability, but what about love All the same They say a life without love is no life at all, so what am I living. Am I to work my life away without someone to love? There must be more to life than medals, love must be more. Love is enough
